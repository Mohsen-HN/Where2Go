Where2Go 🗺️

Welcome to the Where2go challenge on AIcrowd!

Challenge Description:

Traveling and tourism are getting more and more attention lately, specially ever since the end of Covid-19. People are traveling a lot and with the uprising of social media, they are often sharing their trip experiences and pictures. This allows other users to discover maybe new places and sometimes even motivates them to plan a trip to those locations. Unfortunately, to do that, we have to know where those pictures are located.

In this exciting crowdsourcing project, we aim to develop an AI solution to accurately geolocalize images of Swiss cities posted on social media. By exploiting the power of the crowd and the capabilities of artificial intelligence, we aim to build a comprehensive dataset of Swiss city images and their corresponding locations.

Problem Statement:

The task at hand is to create an AI model that can predict the name of the Swiss city in which an image extract from a social media. The input to your model will be an image, and your model should output for now* the name of the city in which it is located.

Challenge Impact:

The Where2Go challenge addresses the task of geolocalizing images of Swiss cities posted on social media. By determining the geographical location of these images, we can gain valuable insights into various aspects, such as tourism patterns, popular landmarks, and urban development. The potential impact of this challenge extends to multiple domains:

  1. Tourism and Travel: Geolocalized city images can provide valuable information to tourists, helping them explore and navigate cities more efficiently. It can improve their overall travel experience by guiding them to popular attractions and hidden gems. It also can help tourism company to gain massive information about trending location where people will heading for their vacation.
  1. Urban Planning and Development: Analyzing the distribution of geolocalized images can offer insights into the popularity of different areas within cities. This information can aid urban planners and policymakers in making informed decisions regarding infrastructure development, resource allocation, and urban growth.
  1. Social Media Analysis: Geolocalized images can be used for sentiment analysis, brand reputation monitoring, and understanding user preferences. This can be valuable for businesses, marketing agencies, and social media platforms to target specific audiences and improve their services.

The challenge aims to improve the baseline solution by leveraging the power of the crowd and advanced AI techniques. By incorporating crowdsourcing to validate the dataset, we ensure the accuracy and quality of the training data.

Participants are encouraged to explore state-of-the-art deep learning models, such as advanced CNN architectures, attention mechanisms, and transfer learning, to enhance the geolocalization accuracy. Additionally, participants can employ techniques like ensemble learning, data augmentation, and model optimization to further improve the performance of their AI models. The challenge provides a platform for knowledge sharing and collaboration, fostering innovation in geolocalization techniques and potentially surpassing the current baseline solution.

Join the Where2Go challenge and contribute to advancing the field of geolocalization while making a tangible impact on tourism, urban planning, and social media analysis in Switzerland!

Challenge structure:

We have collected a diverse dataset of images from various Swiss cities, sourced from Instagram posts with location tags. The dataset contains a mix of urban landscapes, landmarks, and popular tourist spots from different regions in Switzerland. The dataset has been preprocessed and verified by the crowd to ensure accuracy and consistency.

The dataset is split into two subsets: a training set and a testing set. The training set consists of images paired with their corresponding ground truth location which is the name of the Swiss city in which the picture has been taken. The testing will stay private and will allow us to evaluate all the solution found. It contains input images (not known by your model) on which your model will perform a prediction of the city in which it has been captured. Based on all the prediction done by your model, we will be able to evaluate how accurate it is.

Submission Requirements:

Participants are required to submit their AI model and code, along with any necessary dependencies or instructions for reproducibility. The model should be trained on the provided training dataset and capable of making predictions on any dataset of the same structure as the training set.

Participants need to ensure that their submission adheres to the guidelines and can be executed without any issues. Clear documentation or comments within the code are encouraged to help with better understanding.

Additionally, participants are encouraged to submit a brief description of their approach, highlighting the key techniques and methodologies used in their AI model. This provides valuable insights into their submission and encourages knowledge sharing among participants.
